Easy-care Plants

These plants are perfect for beginners!

Peace lily

Spathiphyllum

Peace lilies loves humidity. If your bathroom gets lots of light, pop it in there. Or mist it every few days to keep it happy.

It's also easy when it comes to light, managing in most conditions, though it’ll flower more in a brighter spot.

And it's pretty tough and will usually bounce back from a missed watering. Water it when the top inch of soil feels dry.

DID YOU KNOW?: In the Chinese art of feng shui, the peace lily is considered an aura purifier. So if your aura is feeling particularly spotless after buying one, that’s why.

Golden pothos

Epipremnum aureum

Golden pothos loves rooms with lots high humidity>, like a bathroom or kitchen. In other rooms, it will appreciate the occasional misting.

Not one to make a fuss, it will be fine in almost any light. You’ll see it grow quicker in a brighter spot.

Super simple to water. Check it weekly and just give it a drink if the top two inches of soil are dry.

TOP TIP: If you notice any stems are looking a bit sad, its really easy to prune. Just use clean scissors to cut it back and you’ll encourage healthy new growth.

Blue star fern

Phlebodium aureum

Blue star ferns love moist air. If you can put it in a bathroom where it's nice and humid, terrific. If not, mist it every couple of days.

It also loves medium light. In the wild, Blue star ferns lives in the dappled light of a forest. So it’s happy in partial shade but loves bright, indirect light.

While it likes a regular watering the soil must only be gently moist, not soggy. Check weekly and give it a drink if the top inch of soil feels dry.

TOP TIP: Fertilising isn’t essential but if you want to give it a boost in spring, give it a light feed with liquid fertiliser diluted to half-strength.
