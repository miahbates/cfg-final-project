Low Light Plants

These plants can live happily in a darker part of your home

Corn Plant

Dracaena fragrans

Corn plants needs humidity to keep looking fresh. She’ll love a bathroom or a spray with a mister every few days.

She's happy in anything from light shade to bright, soft light. Keep her out of harsh direct sun.

She’s very low maintenance on water. Stick a finger in the soil every week or so and if it comes out dry, time for a drink.

TOP TIP: If she’s growing well and you want her to keep getting bigger, consider repotting every couple of years. Check our guide to repotting.

Parlour Palm

Chamaedorea elegans

Parlour palms are happy in humid areas of your home. Moisture in the air will help keep its leaves green and fresh. This is easily done by misting it about once a week.

It also loves moderate to bright light, but direct sun is a bit much. In a very sunny room, place your parlour plant away from the window.

In spring and summer, your parlour plant gets thirsty so check it weekly and water it whenever the top inch of soil feels dry.

TOP TIP: Parlour palms are naturally a slow grower but if you want to give it a boost in spring and summer, give it a feed about once a month.

ZZ Plant

Zanzibar Gem

ZZ plants will love you if you feed it with a liquid fertiliser about once a month (no more than that) in spring and summer.

ZZ plants are an ideal option if you don’t have bright rooms. She’ll cope with very little light, though not total darkness.

It also doesn’t need a lot of water, particularly in winter. You can let your ZZ plant dry out completely between drinks.

TOP TIP: Keep it looking its shiny best by giving the leaves a gentle wipe with a soft cloth every now and again. Not often; just when they're looking dusty.
