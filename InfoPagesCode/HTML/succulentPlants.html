Succulents

These cute little plants and incredibly hardy and are experts at conserving water

Jade

Crassula ovata

Jade plants can go up to 3 weeks without watering and don't do well when sitting in moist soil. Let the top 2 inches of water dry out between watering.

They also need lots of light so are best placed in an area of the home that gets up to 4 hours of direct sunlight everyday.

Jade plants don't cope well with cold. The temperature of most homes should be fine, but keep them away from draughts.

TOP TIP: Keep it looking its best by using a fertilizer on your Jade plant about once every six months.

Aloe vera

Aloe vera

Aloe vera plants prefer warmer areas of the home, so keep away from draughts.

They’ll enjoy a spot with lots of bright light, though not too much direct sun. It won’t enjoy living in shade.

Its leaves hold a lot of water, so it doesn’t need frequent watering. Water only when the soil is dry.

TOP TIP: All the water in those big leaves mean its a pretty heavy plant, so it’ll need a sturdy pot to avoid tipping over.

Echeveria

Echeveria spp.

Echeveria plants prefer soil with excellent drainage so they don't sit in wet soil for too long.

They’ll enjoy a spot with lots of bright light so won’t enjoy living in shade.

Its leaves hold a lot of water, so it doesn’t need frequent watering. Let the soil completely dry out before watering your Echeveria again.

TOP TIP: Your Echeveria will always try to grow towards the light, so turn your plant every few days to keep growth even.
