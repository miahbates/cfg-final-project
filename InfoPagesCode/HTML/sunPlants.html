Sun-loving Plants

These plants love to bask in the sunshine so put them in the sunniest part of your home!

Fiddle-leaf fig

Ficus lyrata

The Fiddle Leaf Fig is a huge fan of high humidity areas. You’ll see the healthiest growth if you give the leaves a regular mist. It absorbs a lot of moisture through the leaves.

Those big leaves like a lot of indirect light, so put it somewhere bright, e.g. away from the window in a sunny room.

Your Fiddle Leaf Fig is much thirstier in summer than winter. Check it once a week and water if the top two inches of his soil are dry.

TOP TIP: Don’t worry if he drops a leaf or two after delivery while adjusting to his new home. It’s totally normal. He may also drop a couple in winter.

Snake plant

Sansevieria trifasciata

If you want to be very nice, give your Snake plant a liquid feed (diluted to half strength) once in spring and again in summer.

It’ll be fine in pretty much any light conditions, except total darkness. It’ll grow best in bright, indirect light.

It’s very drought tolerant so let it dry out between waterings. In winter, it’ll only need water once a month or so.

TOP TIP: You can keep him extra happy by wiping his leaves whenever they get a bit dusty. It helps him breathe more easily.

Chinese money plant

Pilea peperomioides

It’ll be over the moon if you feed it once per month in spring and summer. No need in winter.

The Chinese money plant loves a spot with lots of light. It’s not keen on harsh direct sunlight so place it slightly away from windows.

It’s easy-going when it comes to water. It’ll only need a drink when you can feel the soil is dry to the touch.

TOP TIP: It grows toward the light. It’s not essential, but you can keep it growing nice and evenly by rotating the pot every few days.
